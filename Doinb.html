Biography

Kim "Doinb" Tae-sang (Hangul: 김태상) is a League of Legends esports player, currently mid laner for FunPlus Phoenix.

Doinb is a high-elo solo queue mid laner from Korea. Even praised highly by the likes of Faker, he was recognized as a solo queue talent before being picked up by QG in the middle of the LSPL 2015 Spring Split debuting his skill on Katarina. QG were invited to IEM Katowice in March 2016 where they beat Fnatic in their first best of one but were then subsequently eliminated in the group stages following a loss to SK Telecom T1 and then losing to Fnatic 1 - 2. In May 2016, Qiao Gu Reapers renamed to Newbee.[20] Doinb was quickly moved to Newbee Young after there was internal conflict within the team between him and Swift. The team eventually fought their way through the LSPL and qualified for the LPL. Per LPL rules they were re-branded into the QG Reapers in November of 2016. QG placed 3rd in their group in the 2017 LPL Spring Season however in the playoffs they lost 2-3 to I May in the first round. JD Gaming acquired the roster and LPL spot of QG in May of 2017 with Doinb also joining the newly formed squad. Their first showing was at the 2017 Demacia Cup but it wasn't particularly good as they placed 9th-12th after an 0-2 loss to LGD Gaming. In the 2017 LPL Summer Season JD Gaming finished 5th in Group B with 6 wins and 11 losses which eliminated them from playoff contention. They also placed 2nd at the National Electronic Sports Tournament 2017.


Trivia

  • Famous for his Ryze, Nautilus, Viktor, Kled, Karma, and Vladimir.
  • Known for his off-meta tank and brusier picks and having a high win-rate with those champions, with his play being focused on assisting his teammates rather than dominanting lane.
    • LPL casters termed these picks "dark technology", a term that was first used internationally by Smeb at Rift Rivals 2018, before the term took over after his successful Worlds run.
  • Known as a skillful Katarina player in KR server before the champion got reworked by Riot.
  • MVP of the LPL 2017 Spring Regular Season.
  • Speaks Chinese fluently.
  • Gained Chinese residency in 2019, a rare feat for a foreign player as the LPL has some of the strictest residency rules.
  • Is married to Li "Umi" You-Zi, a Chinese former caster of Dungeon Fighter Online.
    • Proposed to her publicly in 2018 after joining FunPlus Phoenix.
  • Reached 1,000 kills in the LPL in February 2019.
    • The 16th player to reach 1,000 kills in the LPL.
